# Business Memo: Customer Churn Risk Investigation

## To: Product, Marketing, and Customer Support Teams

## Subject: Key Customer Retention Risks Before Campaign Launch

Based on exploratory analysis of customer, order, support, web engagement, and intervention data, several churn-related patterns were identified that should be investigated before launching retention campaigns.

### 1. Low Customer Engagement Signals Higher Churn Risk

Customers with lower website/app activity, fewer sessions, reduced product views, and longer gaps since the last visit appear more likely to churn.

**Recommended Action:**
Prioritize re-engagement campaigns for inactive users using reminders, personalized recommendations, or browsing-based offers.

---

### 2. Support Friction May Contribute to Churn

Customers with more support complaints, unresolved issues, negative sentiment, or reopened tickets show elevated churn risk.

**Recommended Action:**
Review customer-service workflows, especially for refund delays, late deliveries, and repeated complaints.

---

### 3. Weak Purchase Frequency Indicates At-Risk Customers

Customers with lower purchase frequency and reduced spending behavior appear more likely to churn compared to repeat purchasers.

**Recommended Action:**
Design loyalty or replenishment campaigns targeting low-frequency customers.

---

### 4. Loyalty Participation Appears Valuable

Customers enrolled in loyalty programs may demonstrate lower churn tendencies than non-enrolled users.

**Recommended Action:**
Encourage loyalty signups for customers showing declining engagement.

---

### 5. Campaign Effectiveness Requires Investigation

Certain intervention strategies may not perform equally across customer groups.

**Recommended Action:**
Evaluate whether campaign types are aligned with customer behavior and engagement patterns instead of applying broad discounts to all users.

---

## Priority Recommendation

Before increasing retention spend, the company should prioritize understanding disengaged users, customers with negative support experiences, and low-frequency purchasers. A targeted strategy is likely to be more cost-effective than blanket discounting.

Retention efforts should focus on behavior-backed customer segments rather than treating all churn risks equally.
